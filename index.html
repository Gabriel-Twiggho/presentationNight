Executive Summary

This analysis treats the AI ecosystem as a consolidated entity, aggregating 15 seed companies with their material relationships. Valuation Assessment

The bubble appears overvalued relative to current revenue

While the 16.9x EV/Revenue multiple aligns with Big Tech comparables, it masks three risks:

  • AI revenue growth is decelerating (47% ‚Üí 38%)
  • The AI-only multiple of 48.8x assumes sustained hyper-growth
  • Nvidia's 34.4x multiple implies perpetual GPU dominance despite rising competition

Methodology & Sources

Data Collection Approach

Public Companies: SEC filings (10-K, 10-Q) from EDGAR for Nvidia, Microsoft, Oracle, AMD, and Intel.

Private Companies: Best available estimates from Financial Times, Wall Street Journal, Bloomberg, Reuters, and TechCrunch with confidence scores and ranges.

Consolidation Methodology

Outside Revenue = Total Revenue - Internal Revenue

Removed $31.93B of bubble-to-bubble payments (2025 LTM) to avoid double-counting:

  • Chip sales: Nvidia to Microsoft/Oracle/CoreWeave ($22.4B)
  • Cloud services: OpenAI/xAI to Microsoft Azure ($2.25B)
  • Processors: AMD/Intel to Microsoft/Oracle ($6.75B)
  • API services: Anysphere to OpenAI ($30M)

Conclusion

The AI bubble demonstrates robust growth and justifies a premium valuation, but multiple expansion has outpaced revenue growth by 2x, creating downside risk if growth decelerates. The bubble is highly concentrated (84% in Nvidia + Microsoft), making it vulnerable to a public tech correction but resilient to private AI revaluations.

The Bubble is Priced for Perfection

With AI revenue growth already slowing from 47% to 38% and competition intensifying, a 20-30% correction is plausible if revenue growth normalizes to 10-15%.

Monitor: (1) Nvidia's pricing power, (2) Microsoft's AI monetization, (3) Private unicorn IPO valuations.
