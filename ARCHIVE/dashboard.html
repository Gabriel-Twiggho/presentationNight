Executive Summary

The AI bubble demonstrates strong fundamentals with revenue growth of 14.3% supporting EV growth of 19.1% in 2025 LTM. The EV/Revenue multiple of 14.04x reflects the high-growth nature of AI businesses and is within the range of enterprise software multiples (10-15x) but above Big Tech averages (8-12x).

Key findings: Microsoft and Nvidia dominate, contributing 48.8% and 18.6% of revenue respectively. AI-attributable revenue growth of 26.4% is significantly higher than the full-company rate, suggesting the AI segment drives disproportionate value creation. Private companies like OpenAI ($300B EV, $12B revenue = 25x) command premium valuations reflecting growth expectations.

Conclusion: The bubble appears reasonably valued given growth rates, though sustainability of 15-25% annual growth is critical. If AI revenue growth slows below 15%, the 14x multiple would look expensive compared to mature tech at 8-10x. Investors should monitor whether revenue growth can sustain premium multiples as the market matures.
